What is the relationship between AstraEditor and TurboWarp/other editors?

The content is based on personal opinions

Overall Relationship

In summary, TurboWarp is a deeply modified (forked) version of Scratch. Other derivative editors, including AstraEditor, are in turn modified from TurboWarp.

Because TurboWarp is open-source, these derivative projects do not constitute "plagiarism" of one another.

Plugins & Extensions

Most of these editors maintain their own repositories for extensions and plugins. It is also common for them to integrate extension libraries from other editors.

Typically, plugins are added upon submission by their developers. I am not fully certain of the specific open-source licensing terms governing all plugins, so I will not go into detail here. The key point is this: there is no need to be surprised if you see the same plugin or extension available in different editorsâ€”this is a normal part of the ecosystem.

China

The Scratch-based coding scene in China is relatively niche. Currently, the main editors with significant presence in China are: Scratch, TurboWarp, and CodeMao.

CodeMao is a heavily customized fork of Scratch (details beyond that are not relevant here). Our focus remains on TurboWarp.

TurboWarp and Its Derivatives in China

The most notable TurboWarp-based editors in the region are Gandi and 02Engine. Gandi is tailored for the CCW community, while 02Engine is developed by 02Studio. Neither is affiliated with AstraEditor.

In practice, you will often find the same extensions or plugins across different editors. This can happen either by the choice of the extension developer or by the editor developer's initiative to include it. Unless stated otherwise, this does not imply unauthorized use or "theft."

AstraEditor's Position

To be clear, AstraEditor itself is one such derivative of TurboWarp. It exists alongside other forks like 02Engine and PenguinMod.
